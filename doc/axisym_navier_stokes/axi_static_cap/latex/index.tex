\chapter{Demo problem\+: The (axisymmetric) static free surface bounding a layer of viscous fluid.}
\hypertarget{index}{}\label{index}\index{Demo problem: The (axisymmetric) static free surface bounding a layer of viscous fluid.@{Demo problem: The (axisymmetric) static free surface bounding a layer of viscous fluid.}}
\hypertarget{index_overview}{}\doxysection{\texorpdfstring{Overview of the problem}{Overview of the problem}}\label{index_overview}
We consider an open circular container of unit radius containing a still viscous fluid of prescribed volume $ \mathcal{V} $ that meets the wall of the container at a contact angle $ \theta_{c} $. The problem is extremely similar to that described in the ~\newline
 \href{../../../navier_stokes/static_single_layer/html/index.html}{\texttt{ two-\/dimensional static cap tutorial. }} The exact solution corresponds to a free surface of constant curvature that is the arc of a circle rotated about the axis to give a section of a sphere. The mean curvature of the interface in this problem is $ \kappa = 2/r = 4\cos\theta_{c}
 $, which differs from the two-\/dimensional problem in which it was $
 2\cos\theta_{c} $.

The only differences between the axisymmetric and the \href{../../../navier_stokes/static_single_layer/html/index.html}{\texttt{ two-\/dimensional }} driver codes are that\+:
\begin{DoxyEnumerate}
\item two-\/dimensional elements are replaced by the equivalent axisymmetric elements;~\newline

\item specified volume is different;~\newline

\item swirl (theta) component of velocity is pinned; ~\newline

\item analytic pressure drop is changed. ~\newline

\end{DoxyEnumerate}In other words we make the following changes\+: \begin{center} \tabulinesep=1mm
\begin{longtabu}spread 0pt [c]{*{3}{|X[-1]}|}

\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item The formula for the specified volume in the axisymmetric case is the true volume $ \mathcal{V} $ divided by $ 2\pi $ because all the axisymmetric equations are divided by the common factor of $ 2 \pi $. The {\ttfamily Axisymmetric\+Volume\+Constraint\+Bounding\+Element} class must be used so that the volume is correctly calculated.
\item The swirl velocity is pinned on the boundaries by including the additional code
