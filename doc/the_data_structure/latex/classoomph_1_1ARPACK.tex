\begin{DoxyCompactList}\small\item\em Solve the real eigenproblem that is assembled by elements in a mesh in a \doxylink{classoomph_1_1Problem}{Problem} object. Note that the assembled matrices include the shift and are real. The eigenvalues and eigenvectors are, in general, complex. Eigenvalues may be infinite and are therefore returned as $ \lambda_i = \alpha_i / \beta_i $ where $ \alpha_i $ is complex while $ \beta_i $ is real. The actual eigenvalues may then be computed by doing the division, checking for zero betas to avoid Na\+Ns. There\textquotesingle{}s a convenience wrapper to this function that simply computes these eigenvalues regardless. Solve the real eigenproblem that is assembled by elements in a mesh in a \doxylink{classoomph_1_1Problem}{Problem} object. Note that the assembled matrices include the shift and are real. The eigenvalues and eigenvectors are, in general, complex. Eigenvalues may be infinite and are therefore returned as $ \lambda_i = \alpha_i / \beta_i $ where $ \alpha_i $ is complex while $ \beta_i $ is real. The actual eigenvalues may then be computed by doing the division, checking for zero betas to avoid Na\+Ns. There\textquotesingle{}s a convenience wrapper to this function that simply computes these eigenvalues regardless. That version may die in NaN checking is enabled (via the fenv.\+h header and the associated feenable function). At least n\+\_\+eval eigenvalues are computed.
